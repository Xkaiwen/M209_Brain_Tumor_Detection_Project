What is the Brain Tumor?

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells in the brain or skull that can be benign (noncancerous) or malignant (cancerous): Brain tumors can develop in many parts of the brain, including the brainstem, sinuses, nasal cavity, and skull base.

Importance of early detection

The brain is the central organ that regulates essential functions such as memory, cognition, sensory of vision, touching and hearing. Any abnormal growth or disruption in the brain significantly impacts these functions, making early detection critical.

Brain tumors are among the top 10 causes of death globally.

Early identification offers:1. Improved chances of successful treatment. 2. Reduced risk of permanent neurological damage. 3. Enhanced quality of life for patients.
